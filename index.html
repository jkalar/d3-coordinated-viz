Voter turnout is a rate that indicates how many eligible voters voted in an election. Multiple factors can factor into calculations of voter turnout. The rates shown below reflects the total number of ballots cast in a jurisdiction divided by the total number of eligible voters in that jurisdiction. Rates shown are for Presidential and Midterm Elections held between 2000-2018.
